EST. 1894

Historical Greystones Landmark

The Past


The La Touche Hotel in Greystones first opened as the Grand Hotel in 1894 after the arrival of the railway in the middle of the 19th century put the former fishing village on the Victorian tourist map.
The hotel had some 40 rooms, including a billiards and smoking room and a ladies' dining room. A century later, it was the home of Lautrex nightclub, Bennigan's by the Sea, and those iconic 'Pound a drink Thursdays'.


The opening of the hotel was recounted in The Freeman's Journal on June 4, 1894: 'The Grand Hotel, which has been built at Greystones, has just been opened, and on Saturday, a large number of gentlemen, in response to invitations issued by the Directors, attended a dinner given in the hotel in honour of the occasion. The new hotel is built quite near to the sea, so that when the tide is in, the water comes within a few yards of it.'


Michael Collins was one of the guests to frequent the hotel over the years, and he proposed to Kitty Kiernan there on October 8, 1921.




The Present


The former La Touche Hotel in Greystones is now transformed to a striking and luxurious residential development, a combination of apartments and town homes, 14 years after the hotel's closure.
